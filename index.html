Campus Life


Before tumbling down to the iron age, we have gone through ages of revolution. The man first learnt to make fire, cook food. Then the invention of wheel happened, post which it was used for transportation. Using the position of the sun and the stars to determine the time, it was the need of the time to keep progressing and also the never-ending curiosity. The man made tools using whatever material available to him. Here, in all these processes, the nature was the teacher. In today’s age, the source of learning is schools. It is the most-widely accepted form of learning; however, many may argue that you can learn via the internet, but could it really replace a human who has spent his whole life trying to learn something only to teach others? It isn’t a fact unknown that teachers play a vital role in the lives of the students who are placed in their care. They teach them, nurture them, guide them and lead them to a way of becoming a highly-qualified professional and a good human. They are the architects of the future, as the children are the skyscrapers. Teachers mould these children into becoming good humans.
